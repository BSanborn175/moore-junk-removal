Fast, Reliable Yard Cleanup

Limb, Brush, & Landscape Clearing

Pinehurst and Southern Pines are famous for their beautiful towering pines, but storms and seasonal changes can quickly cover your lawns with massive piles of pine straw, fallen limbs, and brush. Standard municipal yard waste pickup is subject to strict size and bundling regulations, and they often won't touch large logs or piles. At Moore County Junk Removal, we pick up any size pile, loading and hauling it in a single visit.

We serve homes and estates in Pinehurst, Southern Pines, Aberdeen, Whispering Pines, and Seven Lakes. Our crew can haul branches, organic logs, brush piles, old mulch bags, soil, and wooden yard features (like fencing, old garden arches, or wooden gazebos).

Compost & Wood Mulch Recycling

We care about environmental sustainability. All clean organic material, branches, and tree parts are taken to local Moore County compost facilities or wood processors to be turned into organic wood mulch and topsoil rather than landfill space.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yard Cleanup FAQs

Will you cut down standing trees?

No. We are a debris removal and hauling company, not a tree removal or arborist service. We do not fell trees or prune standing branches. However, once a tree has been cut down or trimmed by property owners or arborists, we can load and haul the remaining logs and branches.

What do you do with old wooden fencing?

Old cedar or pine fencing is sorted. Unpainted wood can be composted, while painted or pressure-treated fencing is disposed of at local transfer stations in accordance with environmental regulations.

Can you haul dirt, soil, or sod?

Yes, we can haul bags of sod, topsoil, and minor loose dirt piles. Because soil is extremely heavy and dense, these loads are priced by weight and access logistics, rather than standard volume pricing.
