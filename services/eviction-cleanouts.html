We work around your schedule, can be on-site the same day you call, and provide photo documentation so you don't have to be present.

North Carolina Law and Tenant Property

Under North Carolina law, once an eviction order has been executed by the sheriff, tenant property left on the premises may be treated as abandoned. We recommend confirming with your attorney that the legal eviction process is complete before scheduling a cleanout. We do not remove property during an active eviction proceeding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Eviction Cleanout FAQs for Moore County Landlords

How fast can you complete an eviction cleanout in Moore County?

Most eviction cleanouts in Moore County are completed within one business day. We offer same-day scheduling when available and understand that landlords need to turn over rental units quickly to minimize vacancy loss.

What do you do with items left by tenants after eviction?

All items left by an evicted tenant are treated as abandoned property once the legal eviction process is complete. We remove all furniture, clothing, trash, and debris. Usable items may be donated to local Moore County charities. We provide documentation of removed items when requested.

How much does an eviction cleanout cost in Moore County?

Eviction cleanout costs in Moore County typically range from $250 for a small studio to $800+ for a fully furnished 3-bedroom unit. Heavily loaded properties may cost more. We provide a free upfront quote before starting — no hidden fees.

Do I need to be present during the eviction cleanout?

No. Most landlords provide us with a key or lockbox code and let us handle the cleanout independently. We photograph before and after and send documentation directly to you. You do not need to be on-site.

Do you handle eviction cleanouts for commercial properties in Moore County?

Yes. We handle eviction cleanouts for residential rental properties and commercial spaces including retail units, office spaces, and warehouses across Moore County, NC.
