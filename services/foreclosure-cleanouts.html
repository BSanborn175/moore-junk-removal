Frequently Asked Questions

Foreclosure Cleanout FAQs

What is included in a foreclosure cleanout?

A foreclosure cleanout includes removing all furniture, appliances, personal property, and debris left behind by the previous occupant. We clear every room, garage, attic, and yard area — leaving the property broom-clean and ready for inspection, repairs, or resale.

How quickly can you complete a foreclosure cleanout in Moore County?

Most standard foreclosure cleanouts in Moore County can be completed within 1 to 2 days. We offer same-day scheduling when available and can accommodate urgent bank and REO timelines that require fast turnaround before appraisals or re-listing.

Do you work directly with banks, lenders, and REO asset managers?

Yes. We work directly with banks, lenders, REO asset managers, and real estate investors who manage foreclosed properties in Moore County. We can provide before-and-after photo documentation and detailed invoices suitable for REO reporting requirements.

How much does a foreclosure cleanout cost in Moore County?

Foreclosure cleanout costs in Moore County range from $350 for a lightly furnished property to $1,200+ for fully furnished or heavily loaded homes. We provide a free upfront quote before any work begins — no surprise fees.

What do you do with items left in a foreclosed home?

All items left in a foreclosed property are treated as abandoned and removed. Usable items in good condition may be donated to local Moore County charities such as the Aberdeen Habitat for Humanity ReStore. All remaining items are disposed of at the Carthage landfill or recycling facilities in compliance with local regulations.
