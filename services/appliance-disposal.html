Safe Extraction & Hauling

Eco-Conscious Appliance Removal

Discarding old appliances is tough. Refrigerators contain harmful refrigerants (Freon) that must be captured by law, and large appliances are simply too heavy for standard curb trash pickup. At Moore County Junk Removal, we carry appliances out of your basement, laundry room, or kitchen, protecting your walls and floors in the process.

We serve residential homes, commercial properties, apartments, and real estate listings in Pinehurst, Southern Pines, Aberdeen, and Carthage. We make sure all metal appliances go to scrap yards or local recyclers, avoiding the landfills completely whenever possible.

Items We Pick Up

We handle refrigerators, chest freezers, washers, dryers, ranges, wall ovens, dishwashers, trash compactors, water heaters, AC units, and commercial cooling equipment. We can take one single appliance or clear out full building decommissions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Appliance Disposal FAQs

Do you require appliances to be unhooked?

Yes. For safety and liability, washers and dryers must be disconnected from the water, drain, and gas/electrical lines before we load. Refrigerators should be unplugged and defrosted (no ice remaining) to prevent leaking on floors.

What happens to the Freon gas in my refrigerator?

We drop appliances off at certified waste transfer stations in Moore County. Licensed professionals recover and recycle the Freon coolant in compliance with EPA clean air regulations before recycling the metal shell.

Do you remove old commercial restaurant appliances?

Yes. We remove commercial kitchen stoves, ranges, stainless steel prep tables, and commercial freezer cases. Call us to coordinate convenient scheduling for your business.
