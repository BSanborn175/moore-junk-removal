The Moore County, NC Disposal Guide: Landfill Hours, Recycling Centers & Donation Sites

By Moore County Junk Removal  ·  May 30, 2026  ·  10 min read  ·  Carthage Landfill · Convenience Centers · E-Waste · Donations

Getting rid of junk in Moore County, NC isn't as simple as leaving it at the curb. Standard residential trash pickup has strict limits on weight, size, and what materials are accepted. Appliances containing refrigerant, electronics, construction debris, and large furniture require different disposal routes — and using the wrong one can result in fines or rejected loads.

This guide covers everything a Moore County resident needs to know: the Carthage landfill's hours and fees, all citizen convenience center locations throughout the county, what the NC Electronics Recycling Law means for your old TV or computer, where to donate usable furniture locally, and what regular trash trucks absolutely will not take.

1. The Moore County Solid Waste Facility (Carthage Landfill)

The primary disposal facility for Moore County is the Moore County Solid Waste Facility located off US-15/501 north of Carthage. This is where most household waste, construction debris, and yard waste ultimately ends up.

4. North Carolina Electronics Recycling (E-Waste)

North Carolina enacted the Electronic Devices Recycling Responsibility Act, which prohibits the disposal of certain electronics in landfills. This affects nearly every Moore County household — especially those with older TVs, computers, or office equipment.

Items banned from NC landfills and trash pickup include:

  • Desktop and laptop computers
  • Computer monitors (CRT and LCD)
  • Televisions (all sizes and types)
  • Printers, fax machines, and scanners

Where to Recycle Electronics in or Near Moore County

  • Moore County Solid Waste Facility (Carthage) — Has designated e-waste drop-off at the facility. Confirm current acceptance with (910) 947-3637 before your trip.
  • Best Buy (Southern Pines area) — Accepts most consumer electronics for recycling at drop-off kiosks, often free of charge.
  • Staples (Southern Pines) — Accepts inkjet cartridges, laptops, and tablets for recycling.
  • Call2Recycle drop-off locations — Search at call2recycle.org for battery and small electronics drop-off spots near Pinehurst and Southern Pines.
If you're clearing an office or large estate with multiple computers, monitors, and printers — Moore County Junk Removal handles e-waste separation and proper recycling routing as part of commercial cleanout jobs.

5. Hazardous Household Waste (HHW) in Moore County

Hazardous household waste includes items that are flammable, toxic, corrosive, or reactive — and they cannot go in regular trash or at standard convenience centers.

Common hazardous items Moore County residents need to dispose of properly:

  • Liquid paint (oil-based or latex): Latex paint that has fully dried/hardened can generally go in regular trash. Liquid latex should be dried out (pour into kitty litter or purchase a paint hardener). Oil-based paint is hazardous and requires HHW disposal.
  • Pesticides and herbicides: Old lawn chemicals, weed killers, and insecticides. Do not pour down drains or on soil.
  • Pool chemicals: Chlorine, algaecides, and pool shock cannot be mixed or disposed of carelessly.
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids: Many Moore County auto parts stores (AutoZone, O'Reilly) accept used motor oil for recycling at no charge.
  • Fluorescent bulbs: Contain mercury. Home Depot accepts CFLs and fluorescent tubes for proper disposal.
  • Propane tanks: Exchange or return to propane supplier. Blue Rhino and AmeriGas locations exist in the Southern Pines area.

Moore County runs periodic Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) collection events where residents can drop off these materials at no cost. Check the Moore County Solid Waste website or call (910) 947-3637 for the current event schedule.

When DIY Disposal Doesn't Make Sense

Driving to Carthage, sorting materials into the right lanes, and paying tipping fees adds up — in time, gas, and fees. For most Moore County residents, it makes financial and logistical sense to hire a junk removal company when:

  • You have more than a few pieces of furniture or junk to move
  • Any items contain refrigerant (fridges, AC units, window units)
  • You're doing a full cleanout — garage, estate, shed, or whole-house
  • The items are too heavy to safely load yourself
  • You don't own a truck or trailer large enough for the load
  • The Carthage landfill tipping fees would eat most of the cost savings from doing it yourself

Quick Answers

Moore County Disposal FAQs

What are the Carthage landfill hours?

The Moore County Solid Waste Facility in Carthage is open Monday–Friday 7AM–4PM and Saturday 7AM–Noon. Closed Sundays. Call (910) 947-3637 to confirm before your visit.

Can I take a refrigerator to the Carthage landfill?

Not directly. Refrigerators contain refrigerant (Freon) and must be handled by a certified appliance recycler or junk removal company with proper certifications. Moore County Junk Removal can remove and properly dispose of fridges, freezers, and AC units — call (910) 420-8159.

Where can I recycle electronics in Moore County?

The Moore County Solid Waste Facility in Carthage has e-waste drop-off (call ahead). Best Buy in the Southern Pines area also accepts consumer electronics at drop-off kiosks. TVs, computers, and monitors are banned from NC landfills under the Electronics Recycling Law.

Where can I donate furniture in Moore County?

The Aberdeen Habitat for Humanity ReStore (1290 Sandhills Blvd, Aberdeen) accepts furniture, appliances, and household goods Tuesday–Saturday 10AM–4PM. The Salvation Army in Southern Pines also accepts furniture and can schedule large-item pickups.
