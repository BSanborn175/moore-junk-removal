The Moore County Waste & Recycling Directory

Carthage Landfill hours, fees, collection sites, and furniture donation options in the Sandhills NC

Getting rid of junk in Moore County, NC, isn't always as simple as leaving it on the curb. Standard municipal trash pickup has strict limits on weight, size, and material. If you have bulky items, old electronics, construction debris, or appliances, you need to know where to take them.

In this guide, we've compiled official directory details, hours, and options for disposing of waste responsibly in Pinehurst, Southern Pines, Aberdeen, and Carthage.

1. The Moore County Landfill (Carthage)

The primary disposal site for household waste, yard waste, and construction debris in the county is the Moore County Landfill.

  • Location: 220 Landfill Road, Carthage, NC 28327 (located off Highway 15-501, just north of Carthage)
  • Phone: (910) 947-3637
  • Hours of Operation: Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM – 4:00 PM, and Saturday 7:00 AM – 12:00 PM. Closed on Sundays.

Common Disposal Fees (Tipping Fees)

Be prepared to pay when you drive onto the scales. Payment is typically accepted via cash, check, or credit card:

  • Municipal Solid Waste (General Trash): Charged by weight (approx. $48+ per ton, with a minimum charge for loads under a certain weight).
  • Construction & Demolition Debris (C&D): Drywall, lumber, siding, and roofing have separate sorting fees (approx. $50+ per ton).
  • Yard Waste & Clean Wood: Stumps, branches, and logs are accepted at a slightly lower rate, provided they aren't mixed with trash.
  • Tires: Passenger tires are accepted, but there may be limits or fees depending on whether you are a county resident with a disposal form.
Important Tip: All vehicles carrying loads to the Carthage landfill must secure their load. Unsecured loads are subject to additional fees or fines.

2. Local Waste Collection & Recycling Centers

If you only have minor household trash, recycling, or small yard debris, you do not need to drive all the way to Carthage. Moore County operates several citizen convenience centers:

  • Pinehurst Collection Site: 40 Dowd Road, Pinehurst, NC. (Convenient for golf course communities and village residents).
  • Seven Lakes Collection Site: 337 Firetree Lane, Seven Lakes, NC.
  • Vass Collection Site: 112 Safety Drive, Vass, NC.
  • West End Collection Site: 605 Love Grove Church Road, West End, NC.
  • Robbins Collection Site: 528 Robbins Road, Robbins, NC.

Note: These collection centers have specific residential load limits and do not accept large commercial trailers, heavy construction debris, or commercial waste.

3. Where to Donate Usable Items in Moore County

If your items are still in good condition, donating them is a great way to keep waste out of the landfill and support the Sandhills community.

Habitat for Humanity ReStore (Aberdeen)

The Habitat ReStore accepts furniture, large appliances, building materials, lighting fixtures, and home decor.

  • Location: 1290 Sandhills Blvd, Aberdeen, NC 28315
  • Phone: (910) 295-7074
  • Donation Hours: Tuesday through Saturday, 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM.

Sandhills Coalition for Human Care (Southern Pines)

Accepts clothing, household items, kitchenware, and smaller furniture pieces.

  • Location: 1500 W Indiana Ave, Southern Pines, NC 28387
  • Phone: (910) 693-1600

4. Items Local Trash Trucks Will Not Accept

Your weekly neighborhood trash service (like Meridian Waste or municipal trucks) will refuse to pick up the following:

  1. Freon Appliances: Refrigerators, freezers, and air conditioners cannot go in regular trash because of refrigerant regulations.
  2. Hot Tubs & Spas: Too heavy and bulky for automated trucks.
  3. Large Furniture: Couches, recliners, and sectionals must be scheduled for special bulk collection (which often incurs fees and long wait times).
  4. Concrete & Brick: Heavy masonry materials will damage standard garbage compactors.
  5. Electronics & E-Waste: North Carolina law bans televisions, computers, monitors, and printers from going into landfills. They must be taken to specialized recycling zones.

If you don't have the truck, the tools, or the time to sort and transport these items to the Carthage landfill or donation centers yourself, hiring a local junk removal service can save you a full weekend of heavy labor.
