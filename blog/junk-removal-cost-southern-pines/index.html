How Southern Pines Pricing Compares to National Chains

National junk removal franchises like 1-800-GOT-JUNK, Junk King, and College Hunks Hauling Junk do serve parts of the Southern Pines/Moore County area — but pricing through franchise operators typically runs 20–40% higher than a locally owned company for the same job.

Why? National franchises have:

  • Royalty fees paid to the corporate franchisor (5–10% of revenue)
  • National advertising overhead baked into local pricing
  • Higher minimum charges to maintain profit margins at scale
  • Less flexibility on pricing for unusual or large jobs

A locally operated company that does most of its business in Southern Pines and Moore County has lower overhead and more pricing flexibility — especially for estate cleanouts, senior downsizing jobs, and large multi-load commercial work.

What Affects the Price for a Southern Pines Junk Removal Job

The five main factors that drive your final price:

  1. Volume: The amount of space your items fill in the trailer. More stuff = higher price. Always the #1 factor.
  2. Material weight: Standard furniture, cardboard, and household goods are volume-priced. Dense materials like concrete, tile, and scrap metal add weight costs on top of volume.
  3. Location in the home: Ground floor or garage items are fastest. Attics, upstairs bedrooms, and basements take longer but are included in standard pricing.
  4. Disassembly: Beds, trampolines, built-in bookshelves, and gym equipment often need to be broken down before they can be removed. Included in quote.
  5. Distance to disposal: The Moore County Solid Waste Facility is in Carthage — about 20 miles from Southern Pines. That drive time is already built into our standard Southern Pines pricing. There's no separate "disposal fee" added at the end.
