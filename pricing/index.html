Pricing Factors

What Affects Your Junk Removal Cost?

Every job is different. Here are the main factors we look at when building your estimate.

1

Volume (Space in the Trailer)

The primary driver of cost. The more space your items occupy in our 16-ft trailer, the higher the price. A single mattress takes very little space; a full attic might fill the whole trailer.

2

Weight of Materials

Heavy materials like concrete, masonry tile, wet soil, and dense scrap metal add weight cost on top of volume cost. Standard household furniture, drywall, and yard waste are all standard volume pricing.

3

Location in the Home

Items on the first floor or garage are fastest to remove. Upstairs bedrooms, basements, attic spaces, or tight closets take extra carry time. We include standard stair navigation in our quotes.

4

Disassembly Required

Hot tubs, sheds, large built-in bookshelves, and gym equipment often require on-site disassembly before they can be moved. Jobs requiring cutting or teardown are priced to reflect this extra labor.

5

Access to the Property

Wide driveways and easy truck access make jobs faster. Gated communities (like Pinewild, CCNC, or Foxfire Village), long driveways, soft grass shoulders, or narrow gates can affect staging logistics.

6

Disposal Type

Most items go to the Moore County Solid Waste Facility in Carthage. Some specialty items (electronics, hazardous materials, FREON-containing appliances) require separate disposal routes, which may add a small fee.

Pricing Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Cost

How much does junk removal cost in Moore County, NC?

Junk removal in Moore County costs $75–$150 for a single item (couch, mattress, appliance), $150–$350 for a partial load, and $350–$600 for a full truckload. Estate cleanouts, hot tub removal, and shed demolition are priced separately based on scope. All pricing is upfront with no hidden fees.

Do you charge by weight or by volume?

We price primarily by volume — the space your items take up in our 16-ft trailer. Dense, heavy materials like concrete, masonry, and soil may incur a weight surcharge since they fill weight limits before volume limits. We explain all pricing upfront before starting any job.

Is there a minimum charge for junk removal?

Yes. Our minimum charge is $75, which covers a small single-item pickup (one mattress, small furniture piece, or a few bags of yard waste). There is no separate trip fee, fuel surcharge, or dump fee added on top of your quoted price.

Do you charge extra for stairs or tight access?

We do not charge a separate stair fee for standard residential removal. Upstairs bedrooms, basements, and attic access are included in the standard quote. Very unusual access situations — like crane-lift only or gated multi-story commercial — are discussed and quoted upfront before any work begins.

How much does an estate cleanout cost in Pinehurst or Southern Pines?

Estate cleanouts in the Pinehurst and Southern Pines area typically cost $400–$900+ depending on the home size and volume of contents. Most 3-bedroom homes run $550–$850. We provide free upfront estimates and can work within probate or real estate timelines. Donation receipts for qualifying items can be provided.

How do I get a fast price estimate?

The fastest way is to text photos of your items to (910) 420-8159. We usually respond with a firm price range within minutes. For larger jobs, we offer free on-site walkthroughs.

How much does hot tub removal cost in Moore County?

Hot tub removal costs $250–$500 in Moore County. The price includes full dismantling on-site, loading all debris, and sweeping the area clean. Gated golf community access (Pinewild, Forest Creek, CCNC) is no issue — we handle those jobs regularly.
